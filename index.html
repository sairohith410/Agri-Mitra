💡 Pro Tips

→Check both top and bottom surfaces of affected leaves — many fungal spores are on the underside
→Note if disease started from field edges or center — helps identify spread pattern
→Look for insect presence — insects often vector bacterial and viral diseases
→Most fungal diseases worsen in humid conditions (RH > 80%)
→Early morning is the best time to observe symptoms clearly

Fertilizer Recommendation

💡 Fertilizer Tips

→Get a Soil Health Card from your nearest Krishi Kendra — it's completely FREE
→Split nitrogen into 2–3 applications — never apply all at once
→Previous pulse crop adds ~30–40 kg N/ha naturally to soil
→Apply fertilizers after light irrigation for better absorption
→Zinc deficiency is the most common micronutrient issue

💧 Irrigation Details

Irrigation Schedule

💡 Water Saving Tips

→Drip irrigation saves 40–50% water vs flood — PMKSY subsidy available
→Irrigate early morning or evening to reduce evaporation loss
→Finger test: push into soil 2 inches — if dry, time to irrigate
→Mulching reduces soil moisture loss by 25–30%
→Critical stages: flowering and grain filling — never miss irrigation then

Soil Health Report

📊 Quick Reference

pH5.5–7.5 ideal for most crops. Below 5.5 = acidic, above 8.5 = alkaline
OCAbove 0.75% is good. Below 0.5% needs organic matter addition urgently
N>280 kg/ha = High · 140–280 = Medium · <140 = Low/Deficient
P>20 kg/ha = High · 10–20 = Medium · <10 = Low
EC>4 dS/m = Saline soil — toxic for most crops

Harvest Planning

Know the perfect time to harvest, post-harvest handling best practices, and storage recommendations to minimize losses.

🌾 Harvest Details
🌾

Harvest Advisory

🌾 Harvest Wisdom

→Harvest in cool morning hours to reduce moisture stress
→Target moisture: Rice <14%, Wheat <12%, Maize <13% for safe storage
→Post-harvest losses can be 15–30% — proper handling is crucial
→Use hermetic storage bags (PICS bags) to prevent storage pests
→Warehouse Receipt System allows loans against stored produce

Organic Farming Guidance

🌿 Organic Basics

→Transition takes 2–3 years — plan your finances accordingly
→Jeevamrutha (cow dung based) is a powerful and cheap biostimulant
→PGS-India (Participatory Guarantee System) is easier than full certification
→Vermicompost increases soil OC by ~0.1% per season
→Certified organic produce fetches 30–50% premium in direct markets
